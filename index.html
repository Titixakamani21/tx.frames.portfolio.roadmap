Day One

Website template +
landing page content
Framer framer.com · free
01Go to framer.com and click Templates in the top navigation
02Filter by Portfolio then filter by Free
03Pick one that is clean, minimal and has a projects section
04Before editing — duplicate it first. Top right → three dots → Duplicate
05Swap all placeholder text with your own content

Your landing page needs only 3 thingsWho you are.
What you do.
How to contact you.

That's it. Do not overthink it.

Evening
Case study that
actually tells a story
UXfolio uxfol.io · free

What is UXfolio?An AI tool built specifically for UX case studies. Unlike ChatGPT, it understands UX. It asks about your research methods, design decisions and process, then builds a professional draft. Like having a senior designer sit next to you.

01Go to uxfol.io and create a free account
02Click Add case study and select the UX methods you used
03Upload a screenshot from your project
04Write 2–3 sentences about the project and the problem you solved
05Let UXfolio generate your first draft
06Edit in your own voice & remove anything generic or robotic
Case study structure
01
Your Role
What did YOU do? Own your work. Don't hide behind "we."
02
Problem Statement
What problem were you solving and for who? Be specific.
03
Research
What did you find before designing? Show thinking, not just output.
04
Solution Overview
What did you design and why is it the right solution?
05
Design Process
Show the messy middle. That's where real thinking lives.
06
Design Philosophy
Why did you make these decisions? This is your POV.
07
Final Design
Your best screens. Clean mockups. Let the work speak.
08
Results / Impact
What changed? Even estimated numbers work.
09
What You Learned
What would you do differently? This shows self-awareness — recruiters love it.

One strong case study beats five weak ones. Start with your best project and do it properly.

Evening
10 details that make your
portfolio look senior
01Write results, not tasks. "Reduced drop-off by 30%" not "Designed onboarding screen."
02Add your tools to every project. Figma, coded it, used AI cause shows you're current.
03Design your error states. Show you think beyond the happy path.
04Add a "my role" section to every case study.
05Remove projects you're not proud of. 2 strong beats 6 weak.
06Make your contact section impossible to miss. Don't make them hunt for your email.
07Add real numbers wherever possible. Users, timelines, results.
08Write your bio in first person and keep it under 3 lines.
09Check every page on mobile. Most recruiters check on their phone first.
10Get one person to read it before you publish. Fresh eyes catch everything.
